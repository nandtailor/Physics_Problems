### **1. Given Constants**
* **Distance ($r$):** $5.3 \times 10^{-11}\text{ m}$
* **Charge of Electron & Proton ($q_e = q_p$):** $1.6 \times 10^{-19}\text{ C}$
* **Mass of Electron ($m_e$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31}\text{ kg}$
* **Mass of Proton ($m_p$):** $1.67 \times 10^{-27}\text{ kg}$
* **Coulomb's Constant ($k$):** $8.99 \times 10^9\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{C}^2$
* **Gravitational Constant ($G$):** $6.67 \times 10^{-11}\text{ N}\cdot\text{m}^2/\text{kg}^2$

---

### **2. Calculating Electric Force ($F_e$)**
Using Coulomb's Law:
$$F_e = k \frac{q_e \cdot q_p}{r^2}$$

Plugging in the values:
$$F_e = \frac{(8.99 \times 10^9) \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19})^2}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$

**Result:**
$$F_e = 8.2 \times 10^{-8}\text{ N}$$

---

### **3. Calculating Gravitational Force ($F_g$)**
Using Newton's Law of Universal Gravitation:
$$F_g = G \frac{m_e \cdot m_p}{r^2}$$

Plugging in the values:
$$F_g = \frac{(6.67 \times 10^{-11}) \times (9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \times (1.67 \times 10^{-27})}{(5.3 \times 10^{-11})^2}$$

**Result:**
$$F_g = 3.6 \times 10^{-47}\text{ N}$$

---

### **4. Finding the Ratio ($F_e / F_g$)**
To see which force is stronger, we divide the electric force by the gravitational force:
$$\text{Ratio} = \frac{8.2 \times 10^{-8}}{3.6 \times 10^{-47}}$$

**Result:**
$$\text{Ratio} \approx 2.3 \times 10^{39}$$
